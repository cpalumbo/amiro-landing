# Amiro Health — Brand Guidelines

## Brand Essence

Amiro is the AI employee that runs patient journeys for clinics. The brand should feel like a calm, capable colleague — not a loud SaaS tool. Every touchpoint should communicate: we make the complex feel effortless.

**Brand personality:** Fresh, innovative, trustworthy, friendly.

**Brand promise:** The operational engine that every clinic deserves.

---

## Logo

The Amiro wordmark uses "Amiro" in the primary typeface with "Health" in a lighter weight or secondary color. The logo should always have clear space around it equal to the height of the "A" on all sides.

Usage rules: always place on solid backgrounds (white, off-white, or deep purple). Never stretch, rotate, or add effects.

---

## Color Palette

The palette draws from the intersection of healthcare trust and modern tech. It's anchored by a rich purple that feels innovative without being clinical, supported by soft neutrals and a warm green accent that signals health and growth.

### Primary

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Amiro Purple | Deep violet | `#7C5BBF` | Primary brand color. CTAs, logo, active states, key UI elements |
| Amiro Purple Light | Soft lavender | `#F3EEFF` | Backgrounds, hover states, tags, subtle highlights |

### Secondary

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Success Green | Emerald | `#10B981` | Positive states, confirmations, health indicators, "live" badges |
| Surface | Cool gray | `#F5F6F8` | Page backgrounds, cards, content areas |
| Text Primary | Near-black | `#1A1A2E` | Headings, body text, high-emphasis content |
| Text Secondary | Mid-gray | `#6B7280` | Captions, descriptions, secondary labels |

### Accent (use sparingly)

| Role | Color | Hex | Usage |
|------|-------|-----|-------|
| Urgent | Soft red | `#EF4444` | No-show alerts, overdue items, errors |
| Warning | Amber | `#F59E0B` | Pending states, attention-needed |
| Info | Soft blue | `#3B82F6` | Informational banners, links |

### Background strategy

Keep backgrounds clean and airy. Default to white (`#FFFFFF`) or off-white (`#F5F6F8`). Use Amiro Purple sparingly for hero sections and emphasis areas — never as a dominant page color. The goal is whitespace and breathing room, similar to the Medfin approach: let the content do the work.

## Typography

### Typeface

**Primary: Inter** — A modern, highly legible sans-serif designed for screens. Used across all headings, body text, and UI elements. Inter communicates friendliness and clarity without sacrificing professionalism.

If Inter is unavailable, fall back to `-apple-system, system-ui, sans-serif`.

### Scale

| Element | Weight | Size | Tracking |
|---------|--------|------|----------|
| Page title (H1) | ExtraBold (800) | 32–40px | -0.02em |
| Section heading (H2) | Bold (700) | 24–28px | -0.01em |
| Subsection (H3) | SemiBold (600) | 18–20px | 0 |
| Body | Regular (400) | 15–16px | 0 |
| Caption / label | Medium (500) | 12–13px | 0.02em |

### Tone in type

Headlines should be direct and human — not salesy. Favor short, clear statements over jargon. Think "Your results are delivered. But is the next visit booked?" rather than "End-to-end patient journey optimization solution."

## Voice & Tone

### Principles

**Talk like a colleague, not a company.** Amiro's voice is the voice of someone who works at the clinic and just happens to be really good at operations. Warm, direct, no fluff.

**Confident but not pushy.** State what Amiro does clearly. Don't oversell. Let the results speak.

**Simple over clever.** If a sentence needs to be re-read to be understood, rewrite it.

### In practice

| Context | Do | Don't |
|---------|------|-------|
| Feature description | "Amiro recovers no-shows automatically" | "Leverage our AI-powered no-show recovery engine" |
| Onboarding | "Let's connect your tools" | "Seamlessly integrate your technology stack" |
| Error state | "Something went wrong. We're looking into it." | "An unexpected error has occurred" |
| Empty state | "No campaigns running yet. Want to start one?" | "No data available" |
| Success | "Done — 12 patients rebooked this week" | "Campaign execution completed successfully" |

### Writing rules

Use active voice. Write in second person ("you" / "your clinic"). Keep sentences short — aim for 15 words or fewer in UI copy. Avoid acronyms on first use. Never say "utilize," "leverage," "synergy," or "end-to-end."

## UI Principles

### Layout

Follow a clean, card-based layout with generous whitespace. Content should breathe. Use a maximum content width of 1200px, with 24–32px gutters between cards and sections.

### Cards & Containers

Rounded corners (12–16px radius). Light borders (`#E5E7EB`) or subtle shadows (`0 1px 3px rgba(0,0,0,0.06)`). White card backgrounds on the off-white surface.

### Buttons

Primary buttons use Amiro Purple with white text and 6–8px border radius. Secondary buttons are outlined or ghost-style. Keep button labels short and action-oriented: "Book now," "Send results," "Start campaign."

### Status indicators

Use colored dots or pills with the semantic palette: green for active/success, amber for pending/warning, red for urgent/error. Always pair color with a text label — never rely on color alone.

### Spacing rhythm

Use an 8px base grid. Common spacing values: 8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 64px. Consistent spacing creates the calm, organized feeling the brand requires.

---

## Imagery & Illustration

### Style

Use clean, minimal illustrations or line art rather than stock photography. If photos are needed, choose real clinic settings with natural light — never staged or overly corporate.

### Iconography

Use a consistent line-icon set (e.g., Lucide or Phosphor) at 20–24px. Stroke weight should match the body text weight for visual harmony. Icons are functional, not decorative — every icon should clarify meaning.

### Product screenshots

When showing the product, use device frames with soft shadows on white or off-white backgrounds. Keep mockups up to date.

---

## Brand in Context

### Patient-facing surfaces

The patient results portal is white-labeled to the clinic's brand, not Amiro's. The only Amiro presence is a small "Powered by Amiro" footer. Keep it subtle — the clinic is the star.

### Staff-facing surfaces

The staff dashboard and portals carry a small Amiro brand presence but are configured per clinic. Use Amiro Purple for interactive elements and navigation. The overall feel should be clean, task-oriented, and fast.

### Marketing & sales

The Amiro landing page and demo generator are where the brand lives most fully. Here, lean into the whitespace, the purple accents, and the confident-but-warm voice. Show the product early. Lead with the problem, not the features.
